Operations on Sets

Master unions, intersections, differences, complements, and the algebra that powers Venn diagrams and probability problems.

Back to Catalogue

Lesson Overview

Sets collect related elements. Union (A U B) captures everything in A or B, intersection (A n B) keeps only the overlap, and difference (A \\ B) removes members of B. Complements depend on the universal set U and flip membership in or out of U.

De Morgan's laws (complement of A U B equals A' n B' and complement of A n B equals A' U B') help simplify expressions and solve questions quickly. Venn diagrams make combined operations visible so you can reason visually and algebraically.

Key Skills

  • Interpret subset notation and cardinality relationships.
  • Apply inclusion and exclusion principles to count elements across sets.
  • Use Venn diagrams and De Morgan's laws to simplify expressions.
