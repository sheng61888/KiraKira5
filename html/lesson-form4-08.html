Measures of Dispersion (Ungrouped Data)

Range, interquartile range, mean absolute deviation, and standard deviation measure how spread out your data really is.

Back to Catalogue

Lesson Overview

Dispersion tells the story of variability. Range compares extremes, IQR captures the middle 50%, and MAD averages absolute deviations from the mean. Variance and standard deviation square deviations before averaging to emphasise outliers.

Understand how adding or multiplying all data values affects each measure so you can predict changes without recalculating everything from scratch.

Key Skills

  • Compute range, IQR, MAD, variance, and standard deviation for raw data.
  • Explain which measures resist outliers best.
  • Describe how shifting or scaling data transforms each measure.
