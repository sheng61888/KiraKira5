Consumer Math: Insurance

Lesson Overview

Insurance is a contract that shifts the possibility of loss from an individual to an insurer. Pandai Form 5 notes focus on identifying the type of risk (life versus general), matching it with the correct coverage, and reading the policy features that decide how much a claim pays. Premiums rise with higher risk profiles or wider coverage because the insurer promises a larger sum assured.

You will interpret real policy snippets, calculate out-of-pocket costs when deductibles or co-insurance apply, and explain why exclusions exist. Linking each feature to its practical effect prepares you for structured and objective questions alike.

Source: Pandai Consumer Math - Insurance.

Key Terms to Remember

  • Risk: the possibility of loss; not every negative event is insurable.
  • Premium: the periodic payment made to secure coverage, influenced by risk level and benefits.
  • Sum assured, deductible, and co-insurance: determine how much the insurer pays versus how much you pay.
