Consumer Math: Taxation

Master Sales and Service Tax (SST) basics, connect taxable goods and services, and apply reliefs to arrive at chargeable income in Malaysia's progressive system.

Back to Catalogue

Lesson Overview

SST has two components: a sales tax applied once at the manufacturing or import stage, and a service tax imposed on specific services such as hotels, prepared food, and telecommunication providers. Personal income tax is progressive, so higher income bands are taxed at higher rates, but reliefs and rebates reduce the chargeable income before tax is computed.

You will read short cases, identify whether SST applies, and practise rearranging the chargeable income formula. Keeping receipts and documentation matters because taxpayers must prove eligibility for reliefs.

Need-to-Know Facts

  • SST stands for Sales and Service Tax; it is not charged at every stage like GST.
  • Tax reliefs and rebates bring down the chargeable income, which in turn reduces tax payable.
  • Progressive systems mean a higher bracket attracts a higher rate, so calculations must be tiered.
