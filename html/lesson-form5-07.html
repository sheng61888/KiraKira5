Measures of Dispersion for Grouped Data

Estimate range, mean, variance, standard deviation, and interquartile range for grouped distributions using class midpoints and cumulative frequencies.

Back to Catalogue

Lesson Overview

Unlike ungrouped data, grouped data require estimating with class midpoints. Mean is computed with the ratio (sum of fx) / (sum of f), while variance uses (sum of fx^2) / (sum of f) minus (mean)^2. Interquartile range draws from a cumulative frequency (ogive) to locate Q1 and Q3 before subtracting. Remember: shifting every value by a constant leaves standard deviation unchanged, but scaling by a factor multiplies it.

You will also reason about skewness (mean vs median) and how outliers push mean and standard deviation more than medians.

Source: Pandai Measures of Dispersion (Grouped Data).

Computation Reminders

  • Use class midpoints when estimating range or calculating the sum of fx and fx^2.
  • Build a cumulative frequency table to read off quartile positions quickly.
  • State the median position ((sum of f) / 2) before interpolating within the relevant class.
