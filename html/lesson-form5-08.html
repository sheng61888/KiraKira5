Mathematical Modelling

Turn real-life stories into equations by defining the problem, stating assumptions, selecting a model, and checking whether your solution makes sense before reporting it.

Lesson Overview

Modelling always starts with clarity: what quantity are we trying to predict and under what conditions? From there, we define assumptions, introduce variables, and choose a mathematical relationship (variation, linear, quadratic, exponential, and so on) that best matches the situation. After solving, we interpret the answer, verify against the context, and refine the model if reality disagrees with the prediction.

The Form 5 notes emphasise using given data to estimate parameters such as the constant k, evaluating residuals to judge the fit, and communicating the process with limitations clearly stated.

Modelling Cycle

  • Define the problem and scope.
  • List assumptions and choose variables or parameters.
  • Build, test, and refine the model before presenting results with context and limitations.
