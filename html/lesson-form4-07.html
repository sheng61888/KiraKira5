Form 4 - Module 07

Graphs of Motion

Translate between distance-time and speed-time graphs so slopes, areas, and motion descriptions all match perfectly.

Back to Catalogue

Lesson Overview

In distance-time graphs, slope equals speed. Flat segments mean resting, steeper segments mean faster travel, and a line sloping down would be impossible.

In speed-time graphs, slope equals acceleration and the area under the curve equals distance travelled. Use rectangles and triangles to split piecewise graphs, then sum the areas for total distance.

Key Skills

  • Describe motion stories from distance-time graphs.
  • Compute distance as area under speed-time graphs.
  • Interpret acceleration from slopes and compare motion segments.
