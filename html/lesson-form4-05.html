Lesson Overview

A graph (or network) is a set of vertices linked by edges. Degree counts how many edges touch a vertex, while paths and cycles describe how we move through the graph. Euler trails use every edge once, Hamiltonian paths visit every vertex once, and trees are connected graphs with no cycles.

Weighted graphs assign distances or costs. Algorithms such as Dijkstra's shortest path or Kruskal's minimum spanning tree help solve routing and optimisation problems quickly.

Key Skills

  • Determine degrees and classify Euler vs Hamiltonian possibilities.
  • Apply rules for existence of Euler trails/circuits and properties of trees.
  • Use shortest-path or spanning-tree ideas to interpret network diagrams.
