Ratio & Graph of Trigonometric Functions

Evaluate sine, cosine, and tangent across 0 to 360 degrees, remember ASTC sign rules, and sketch the core trig graphs with the correct amplitude, period, and asymptotes.

Lesson Overview

This module revisits the unit circle so you can determine exact values (like sin 30 degrees or cos 180 degrees) and infer signs in every quadrant using the ASTC mnemonic ("All Students Take Coffee"). From there, you sketch or read sine and cosine graphs (range -1 to 1, period 360 degrees) and tangent graphs (period 180 degrees with vertical asymptotes).

Scaling the graph with amplitudes or vertical stretches changes only the range, not the period, unless the angle input is multiplied by a factor. Tangent functions produce zeros and asymptotes at predictable intervals, which examiners love to test.

Practice Focus

  • Memorise benchmark values at 0, 30, 45, 60, 90, 180, 270, and 360 degrees.
  • Quote the period and amplitude whenever you describe a trig graph.
  • State where tangent is undefined by listing the angles of its vertical asymptotes.
